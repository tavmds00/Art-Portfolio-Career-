    • Biography

      Tomás Villalobos Moreno is a multidisciplinary artist whose practice spans installation, sculpture, sound, drawing, video, and performance. His father was a jazz musician, composer, and Chicano rights activist who worked with César Chávez and later cofounded Academia de la Nueva La Raza with Tomás Atencio in New Mexico and San Antonio.

      His mother grew up in Japan and Thailand during the postwar period, her father having served as a Japanese Language Officer during the Occupation of WWII. Treating archives as active rather than static, Moreno recomposes fragments of personal and cultural history into spatial and sonic environments where memory is reactivated rather than preserved.

      His work has been exhibited internationally at Casa del Lago, Mexico City, Lugar a Dudas, Cali Colombia, and the Vincent Price Museum, Los Angeles. His writings have been published by Onestar Press and Festina Publicaciones. He is the recipient of UCIRA and UC MEXUS awards.
